Jomplay

UX・IDENTITY・FRONT-END

Backstory and problems

Jomplay was a simple app idea, to book futsal courts seamlessly. What started off as a side project for over two years, gained momentum with the help of two dear friends. The three of us now run the app as a service and are expanding it to more sports and courts in the ASEAN region. The problems we were trying to solve initially:

  1. Booking a court still requires calling or messaging the court staff (subject to human error & poor service).
  2. Organizing a game is tedious with WhatsApp groups and endless back and forth messages.

Visual identity

We envisioned the product as bold and sporty, yet simple. To inspire people to play sports more frequently by making booking and organizing games easy. The identity is a combination of clean bold geometry, edgy street colors, and sports courts. Each of which, reflect the personalities of our target users and the functionality of the product.

Experience design

Make booking simple and painless

One of the biggest issues with booking courts via phone call or messaging is dealing with the person on the other line. More often than not, court staff are rude, uninviting, and re-shuffle bookings to maximize returns. All of which are confusing and unliked by users.

To resolve this, I use a combination of both location and action words throughout the main app flow to orient and direct the user. This creates friendly actionable messaging and ensures that users have a clear path to booking a court.

A common practice among regular players is re-booking their previous game for next week. By providing a simple quick action on the home screen, regular bookers can shorten their usual booking flow down to a single tap.

Teams and open games for fast invites

- issue with organiation and player commitment/canclellation how we solve that - easy to leave join, find new players After making booking users can invite friends to app. Regular players typically play with same people. Therefore make sense to create groups. That can be invited with a single tap instead of tap on multiple users one by one.
