2019 – ONGOING

Jomplay

STORY

Jomplay was a simple app idea, to book futsal courts seamlessly. What started off as a side project, gained momentum with the help of two friends. The three of us now run the app as a service and are expanding it to more sports and courts.

SCOPE + PROBLEM

The idea started from a group of futsal players that were upset about how their court bookings were consistently being mis-managed. We realized that there was an opportunity to provide a service that would make booking sports courts pain-free.

In addition to bookings, we wanted to provide features that helped users organize their games, pay cashless and find new players easily. Here are three of the key problems we looked to tackle in our initial MVP release.

01. Tedious bookings

Booking a court still requires calling or messaging the court staff which is often subject to human error & poor service.

02. Difficult to organize

Organizing a game using WhatsApp groups with endless back and forth messages isn't optimal.

03. Look clean, yet sporty

We wanted a visual identity that captured the sportiness of our target audience, but that was also clean and simple for our users to understand.

PROCESS – VISUAL IDENTITY

Emphasis on clean bold geometry throughout the visuals was key to obtaining a modern look that felt sporty and remained un-tacky.

01. Typography

Geomanist was chosen as the primary typeface for this project as it combined the perfect amount of rounded-ness while still providing clean sharp edges throughout multiple weights. Both roundness and sharp edges we're a key component in representing sports and courts. And as a bonus, the ultra-italic weight was absolutely perfect for use in our social media, to capture our users attention.

02. Color

I explored various sporty themes in my search for a functional and sporty color pallete that still kept in line with our idea of being minimal. A punchy green proved itself to be the primary color for our interface main CTA's. While grey's were used liberally to impart the feeling of edgy street sports – a common theme among our target audience with a sporty lifestyle.

03. Iconography

To avoid confusing users with many icons (abstract symbols), I made it a guiding principle to use words wherever possible. Especially in areas with key actions. As such, icons are used sparingly in this app. For the few that there are, I simply ensured that their construction was aligned with our typography – bold, edgy and geometric.

04. Brandmarks

The wordmark started off with just the word "jomplay". After getting some user input we found that it didn't convey the emotions we wanted. Our identity felt more like we'd built a task manager over a sports app. I then began to explore the notion of the court itself as the primary marker for our identity – we are a sports court booking app after all.


What finally emerged were the very court lines that basically convey the entire existence of the court. Boundaries built by humans for contained explosions of fun and skill. And so, the notion of pushing yourself till the edge of the court infused itself into our mark, bringing the elements of motion and dynamicity that were missing from before.

OUTCOME + CREDITS

This was a really exciting, fun and personal project for me. Having been the first working product I designed, it holds a special place in my heart.

As the current Chief Design Officer for Jomplay, I'm always excited to take it further. And although I can't always provides updates for it here, the next steps for it are so much clearer, having literally built it from the ground up. Here are some of my important takeaways from this project:


Deliver early value

Getting the product early to market, would have reaped its own rewards by allowing us to conduct more research and testing with real users. Even if that meant not having certain features.

Document and plan from the start

I learned that figuring out why, and writing it down, is probably the best thing you can do for yourself when making design decisions. Keep tabs on why you made one choice over another.
